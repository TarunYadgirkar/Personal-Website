### GuestFlow — Agentic arrival orchestration for luxury hotels
- **Problem:** Personalizing a returning hotel guest's arrival normally requires manual staff work
  per reservation.
- **What I built:** An agentic pipeline that, the moment a reservation lands, autonomously assembles
  a bespoke arrival — pulling guest history, live flight status, timezone/climate delta, booking
  metadata, and origin-country tech profile, then applying four lenses (Guest Purpose, Party
  Composition, Cultural Nuance, Tech Continuity) to produce three artifacts: a Staff Pre-Arrival
  Brief, a Room & Amenity Spec, and a guest-facing pre-loaded itinerary. A Confidence + Consent
  layer only auto-acts on high-confidence, non-invasive inferences and routes borderline calls to
  staff.
- **Stack:** TypeScript · Anthropic API (Claude) · ElevenLabs.
- **Outcome:** Built at Hospitality 2030 — a Rosewood Sand Hill hackathon (May 2026).
- **Links:** GitHub (guestflow). [ASSET: demo screenshot/video]

### VoiceVision — Voice-controlled screen accessibility
- **Problem:** People with visual impairments can't easily reconfigure a screen for their needs in
  real time.
- **What I built:** A voice-driven layer where spoken needs adapt the display live — e.g.,
  "I have red-green colorblindness" applies a deuteranopia filter; "make it dark," "high contrast,"
  "I'm light sensitive," and "macular degeneration" (center magnification) each map to filters that
  stack. Ships with a Manifest V3 Chrome extension that applies the same voice-controlled filters to
  any webpage.
- **Stack:** Next.js (App Router) · TypeScript · Web Speech API · Google Gemini 2.5 Flash (natural
  language → commands) · SVG feColorMatrix · Tailwind · Vercel.
- **Outcome:** 2nd place at the AI Collective Tri-Valley hackathon (Humans in AI Week, Jun 2026).

### CarePath — Voice-first patient navigation
- **Problem:** Patients don't know where to go (ER vs. urgent care vs. doctor vs. home), what it
  costs, or what to bring.
- **What I built:** A voice-first app: speak your symptoms and it recommends a destination with
  visible reasoning and a cost estimate, ending in a shareable Care Card (what to say at check-in,
  questions to ask, red flags). Five connected modes (Triage, Debrief, MedCard, Check-in, Timeline),
  simulated records import (SMART on FHIR / Epic MyChart), and privacy-by-design (data stays in the
  browser).
- **Stack:** Next.js 16 (App Router) · React 19 · TypeScript (strict) · Tailwind v4 · Grok Voice
  (xAI Realtime API) with Web Speech fallback · OpenAI gpt-4o-mini (incl. vision) · Reddit
  arctic_shift API · browser localStorage · Vercel.
- **Outcome:** Built at the Autonomous Healthcare Hackathon (Legion Health × Atlas AI, Jun 2026).
- **Links:** Live https://carepath-five.vercel.app · GitHub https://github.com/TarunYadgirkar/carepath

### Klarity VoiceNote — AI voice intake for mental-health documentation

## BALANCE PAGE (assistive robotics — provisional patent)

- **Problem:** Mobility isn't binary. Most assistive devices (wheelchairs, walkers, canes) solve one
  narrow stage of mobility loss, can accelerate muscle atrophy, and don't adapt across terrain — yet
  the aging population and the gap between lifespan and health-span are growing.
- **Concept:** BALANCE is a hybrid robotic mobility device positioned between simple aids and complex
  exoskeletons, supporting users across the full continuum of mobility with partial-to-full
  assistance.
- **System:**
  - *Assistive Leg Framework* — a dual wheeled-and-leg mobility frame that provides structural
    support and isolates shocks; wheeled movement on flat ground, robotic-leg assistance on stairs
    and uneven/narrow terrain.
  - *Adaptive Control & Navigation Suite* — LiDAR, sonar, 360° cameras, and on-board computing for
    real-time mapping and navigation.
  - Additional features: collapsible hip-centric modular frame, biometric monitoring, lithium
    battery management, and a user interface for adjustable assistance levels.
- **Status:** Provisional patent (No. 63/743,085); design published in the Youth Innovation Journal
  (Fall 2025). Co-developed with a student team.
- **Links:** Paper (Youth Innovation Journal); provisional patent doc (Drive).
